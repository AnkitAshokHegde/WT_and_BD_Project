Income Tax Return (ITR)

About ITR

The assessee submits information about his or her income and related taxes to the income tax department using a form known as an income tax return. ITR 1, ITR 2, ITR 3, ITR 4, ITR 5, ITR 6, and ITR 7 are among the several variants.

Citizens are required to submit returns to the Income Tax Department at the conclusion of each fiscal year under the terms of the Income Tax Act of 1961 and the Income Tax Rules of 1962. Before the deadline, these returns need to be filed. Each Income Tax Return Form is applicable to a specific group of Assessees. The Income Tax Department of India only processes the Forms submitted by eligible Assessees. It is crucial to understand which specific form to use in each situation. Depending on the assessee's category and income source, different income tax return forms are available.

Eligibility Criteria

A person must legally file their income tax returns throughout a financial year if they meet any one of the following criteria:
  • Individuals whose gross annual total income (before any deductions) exceeds Rs. 2.5 lakh, Rs. 3 lakh for senior citizens, or Rs. 5 lakh for super senior people.
  • Those who want to claim an income tax refund.
  • Those who want to carry forward a loss under a head of income.
